# Data Analysis Constitution (Data Pro Max v2)

All analytical processes, Python code, and reports generated by the agent must strictly adhere to the following rules.

---

## 1. Statistical and Quantitative Rigor

*   **Sample Size ($N$) Requirement:** Never present an average, percentage, or correlation without explicitly stating the base sample size ($N = \text{number}$).
*   **Significance & Error Margins:** When discussing differences between groups, always specify the margin of error or the statistical significance (e.g., $p$-value or confidence interval where applicable). Do not declare a difference to be a "trend" or "significant" if the sample size is too small to justify it.
*   **Weighting Integrity:** 
    *   Any weighted analysis must display both the unweighted base size ($N$) and the weighted base size ($N_w$).
    *   Flag any weights that are abnormally high (weight factor $> 3.0$) or low (weight factor $< 0.1$) as they can distort results (Design Effect inflation).
*   **Variable Transparency:** Always state exactly which columns were mapped to compile a metric.

---

## 2. Qualitative Boundaries (On-top of Quantitative)

*   **Prohibition of Percentages:** Never use percentages or proportions when discussing qualitative groups or small samples (e.g., $N < 30$). Instead of "80% of focus group participants complained about pricing", write: "8 out of 10 participants mentioned pricing as a major barrier."
*   **Anecdotal vs. General:** Clearly distinguish between a single *verbatim* quote (anecdote) and a recurring theme. If a theme is mentioned, specify how many participants or transcripts featured it.
*   **Data Anchoring:** Qualitative quotes or themes must be anchored to the quantitative segments defined during `/setup` (e.g., linking specific complaints to the "Detractor" group).

---

## 3. Writing and Synthesis Rules (Tufte Style)

*   **Prohibition of Prose Fluff:** Never use introductory filler phrases. Skip preamble sentences like:
    - *"It is important to note that..."*
    - *"Based on the data provided, we can observe..."*
    - *"An interesting insight from this table is..."*
    - *"As a Lead Research Orchestrator, I analyzed..."*
    - Go straight to the fact: *"NPS is -15, driven by a 45% churn rate in the 18-24 age segment."*
*   **Data Density:** Prefer structured tables and short, high-density bullet points over long prose paragraphs.
*   **Margin Notes (Sidenotes):** Use blockquotes (`> 💡 **Nota de Margem:** ...`) immediately after a data point to place interpretations, qualitative context, or citations. In Tufte layouts, these will be rendered in the side margin.
*   **Conclusion Headers:** Every table must have a conclusion embedded in its header or caption. For example, instead of `### Table 1: Churn by Age`, use: `### Churn concentrated in 18-24 segment (45% of total Churn)`.
